The First Commandment: Know Thy Audience

by Diana Mahmoud

Posted on February 24, 2015 at 9:00 PM

Less you feel like Moses out in a digital desert, defining your target audience—the who you hope to reach via social—must be the very first consideration. You should not even begin to determine what social media channels to utilize, let alone create a single tweet or post, without fully understanding the people or groups of people you are trying to engage.

As a small business owner, who interacts in a very real way with customers every day, the First Commandment should be the easiest to follow. Think about your best clients. What makes their relationship to you and your business so special?

If you have more than one target audience, you should segment them.  Do this by listing them separately and defining their needs as specifically as you can. Include relevant demographical characteristics. Do your best to go beyond the demographics, however. Think about the conversations you’ve had with your clients over the years. In reality, you have listened and engaged them well enough to describe who these people are in a way that will benefit your digital strategy, too.  Where do they live? What do they care about? What and whom occupy their days? Where are they online and offline? What are their business or seasonal patterns and behaviors? What is it about your products and services that interest them? These are just a few of the substantive questions you can answer and include in your target audience.

A follower on Twitter or Instagram, a like on Facebook, a subscription to your blog—these are not things. They are people. As digital marketers, we need to honor them and treat them with respect or we just might lose them. To do that, we need to know and care about who they are.

Know Thy Audience. It’s the first and fundamental step in creating a successful strategy.
