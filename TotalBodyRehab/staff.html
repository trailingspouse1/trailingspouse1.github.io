Total Body Rehab Staff

Mary Beth Klemm, PT

The owner of Total Body Rehab, Mary Beth is a licensed physical therapist who just celebrated her 32nd year in the physical therapy profession. Mary Beth brings an eclectic approach to TBR with a strong focus on manual treatment.

She received her undergraduate degree at Montclair State University in New Jersey and earned her degree in Physical Therapy at the University of Pennsylvania in 1980.

Mary Beth has extensive experience in orthopedics and sports medicine, both in the hospital and private outpatient settings, and has worked as a physical therapist in New Jersey, Pennsylvania and North Carolina.

Mary Beth grew up in Clifton, NJ and relocated to Cary in 2000. She has 2 daughters and a son and enjoys staying active with her husband. Hiking, biking, performing Yoga and Pilates help keep her fit.

Kelley Wiedmeyer-Davis, MS,PT

Kelley brings over 16 years experience to Total Body Rehab. She has been working exclusively in outpatient orthopedic clinics in Pittsburgh and Cary since she graduated with her Master’s Degree in Physical Therapy at Washington University in St. Louis in 1995.

Kelley’s continuing education has focused on many classes with Paul St. John Neuromuscular Therapy Pain Relief for the extremities, neck, low back and jaw musculature. She has also taken extensive courses for the treatment of spinal dysfunction and SIJ Dysfunction with Mulligan, Maitland, and Stanley Paris. Kelley has taken several spinal stabilization courses including Pilates Mat Certification through Power Pilates, and Evidence-Based Approach to Spinal Segmental Stabilization. Other courses have focused on the neck/scapular region, shoulder, hip, knee and foot/ankle. She enjoys performing video evaluations of running and walking gaits to better identify movement dysfunction and better educate the patient. She has also taken specialty course work in Craniosacral Therapy and Temporal Mandibular Joint Dysfunction and has found incredible benefits with these techniques for specific cases. She is a firm believer in comprehensive patient education to help patients maintain progress long after they have been discharged. She believes the more a patient understands about the biomechanical forces of the body, the better equipped they will be in avoiding painful movements in the future.

Kelley grew up in Iowa and stayed busy playing basketball, volleyball, and softball. She and her husband relocated to Cary in 1999. She enjoys helping coach and watch her daughter’s sports teams as well as teaching them the importance of leading an active lifestyle. Kelley enjoys running, weight training and reading about health related topics.
